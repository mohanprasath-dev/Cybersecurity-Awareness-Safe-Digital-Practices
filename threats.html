Major Threat Categories

Familiarize yourself with the most prevalent types of cyber attacks targeting users today.

Phishing Attacks

Deceptive emails, messages, or websites designed to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. Phishing remains the most common attack vector, accounting for over 80% of reported security incidents.

Malware

Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems. This includes viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and adware. Malware can steal data, encrypt files, or turn your device into a bot for larger attacks.

Ransomware

A devastating form of malware that encrypts your files and demands payment for the decryption key. Ransomware attacks have increased dramatically, targeting individuals, businesses, hospitals, and even government agencies with demands ranging from hundreds to millions of dollars.

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation techniques used to trick people into making security mistakes or giving away sensitive information. This includes pretexting, baiting, quid pro quo, and tailgating—all exploiting human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Identity Theft

The fraudulent acquisition and use of someone's personal information for financial gain or other malicious purposes. Criminals use stolen data to open accounts, make purchases, file taxes, or commit other crimes in your name.

Man-in-the-Middle Attacks

Attackers secretly intercept and potentially alter communications between two parties who believe they're directly communicating with each other. Common on unsecured Wi-Fi networks, these attacks can capture login credentials, financial information, and private messages.

Recognizing Phishing Attempts

Phishing emails have become increasingly sophisticated, often perfectly mimicking legitimate communications from trusted organizations. Learning to spot the warning signs can save you from falling victim.

Always verify the sender's email address carefully—attackers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but contain subtle differences. Be wary of urgent language designed to pressure you into immediate action.

  • Suspicious sender addresses with misspellings
  • Urgent requests for sensitive information
  • Links that don't match the displayed text
  • Generic greetings instead of your name

Emerging Threats

Stay ahead of the curve by understanding the newest threats on the horizon.

AI-Powered Attacks

Cybercriminals are leveraging artificial intelligence to create more convincing phishing messages, deepfake content, and automated attacks that can adapt to security measures in real-time.

IoT Vulnerabilities

As more devices connect to the internet—from smart home gadgets to medical devices—they create new entry points for attackers who exploit weak security in these often-overlooked devices.

Supply Chain Attacks

Attackers target software providers and third-party services to compromise multiple organizations through a single breach, making these attacks particularly devastating and difficult to detect.
